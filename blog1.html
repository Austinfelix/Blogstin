Once upon a time...

I was as usual watching TV-shows and procrastinating until it hit me that I do not have any idea what am I going to make of myself in the future?
I kept wandering into my thoughts after hearing all the podcasts that were rumored to help me find my passion in life, couldn't find anything. So I went up to my mother and asked her, what she felt is the most suitable career for me?

Oh I forgot to tell you, I am 18 years old by the way

I am currently studying Hotel Management in Chennai.
So anyways my Mother was really not expressive about the question I asked her but she directly gave me an answer right in split seconds and she said that, "I always wanted you to become a DOCTOR!".
Now that's a buzzkill because:-
A. I have no interest in becoming a doctor.
B. I am not that smart enough.

So still my problem wasn't solved yet, I was trying everything in my power to find my passion, then after watching several different movies and TV shows I went on YouTube to look on the ways to find your passion?
For some reason everyone kept recommending different books to read , So I went to a place where they won't recommend books and that place is called TED-X. There was this smart guy who kept talking about asking ourselves 3 questions which if we answer will lead us to finding our passion, those 3 questions were:- 1. What are you good at?(Obviously) 2. What excites you in life? 3. What is it that you could do to help society in a big way? To be honest, even after answering those questions I couldn't find my passion for real so I kept looking, again and again until the time I just got really tired and slept off.

Anyhow I still continued watching a lot of movies until I saw this one particular movie on Netflix called "The Social Network" directed by David Fincher, turns out watching Mark Zuckerberg coding in the movie made me want to code too.
So I immediately stopped watching the movie and started doing some research and started learning how to code using some free online tools like codecademy, youtube, google and some E-books. Its been at least a week since I have started coding and I started learning HTML and now I have completely finished learning it and currently moving on to learning CSS now. Now I have a goal in my mind to learn web development and earn money through freelancing to buy myself a Mac book Air. Sounds like a big goal for a 18 year old stuck at home during quarantine, I hope to accomplish it! Someday. Until then Ta-ta!
